The Doctor's Mindset

As a psychiatrist, your job isn't just to prescribe medication. You must:

  • Listen carefully to patient stories
  • Observe symptoms and behaviors
  • Consider multiple diagnoses before deciding
  • Weigh treatment benefits vs. side effects
  • Build trust with your patients

The Diagnostic Process

1

Gather Information

Read patient history, chief complaints, and observe behavior

2

Identify Symptoms

Note duration, severity, and impact on daily life

3

Differential Diagnosis

Consider multiple conditions that match the symptoms

4

Confirm Diagnosis

Use DSM-5 criteria to make your final diagnosis

Treatment Decisions

Every treatment has benefits and risks. Consider:

Medications

SSRIs, antipsychotics, mood stabilizers - each has specific uses and side effects

Therapy

CBT, DBT, psychotherapy - essential for long-term recovery

Lifestyle

Exercise, sleep, social support - foundational to mental health

Managing Your Clinic

  • Earn Money: Successfully diagnose and treat patients
  • Buy Equipment: Better diagnostic tools improve accuracy
  • Purchase Books: Medical knowledge unlocks new treatments
  • Upgrade Clinic: Attract more patients and increase fees
  • Manage Time: Each consultation takes time - balance quality and quantity
